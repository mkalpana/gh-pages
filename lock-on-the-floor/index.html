Lock on the Floor

By RedEagle3
November 5, 2020
0

Before we start, I do not own The Evil Queen.

-RedEagle3

------------------------

An alarm made James jump off the sofa and fall face-first into the hard, cold, wooden floor. He groaned in agony, it was Halloween, the one night he couldn’t sleep because of the chance of sighting an actual ghost or ghoul. He did it every Halloween and regretted it every Halloween because of the painful cycle of falling sleeping and falling onto the floor, most of the time, face-first.

Rolling onto his back, he massaged his aching nose and picked up the glasses that had slid off his face while he was sleeping before tapping the alarm into silence. He stood up and stretched his sore back. After a fine assessment of all his multiple throbbing injuries, he went to the kitchen to grab an icepack and held it to his nose.

Two slow knocks sounded at his door.

Glancing at the kitchen clock, he wondered why someone would be trick or treating so late on Halloween.

Two more impatient knocks reverberated through the door.

“Comming!” He called out to his unexpected visitor as he grabbed some candy and rushing to the door. As the door swung open, there was no living thing in sight accept a red-eyed rat which scuttled across the verandah at the sight of him.

Scratching his head he wondered what to do next. Was the rat secretly Pettigrew? If the rat was the only thing here, then who knocked? The only trace of a living thing other than that rat was the lock of hair on the floor of the veranda. It looked to be around a meter long, it was red at one end before turning into a night black. Maybe a trick or treater that had passed by before? That still didn’t explain the knock he’d heard just now. This seemed an awful lot like the plot of a horror movie.

on Halloween night, a boy called James is all alone in his house. He hears a knock at the door and wonders who would be trick or treating so late in the night. He opens the door and sees a strand of hair half hie height. What will happen next?

Pushing away that thought, he went back to the sofa when a phantom chill passed through his body. Goosebumps slowly coved his arms and he grabbed a thick cloth in an attempt to warm up. It was almost like his bones were made of chilled copper, heavy as lead as if his essence itself was frozen. The chill crept through his body until his entire existence felt like his arms.

He could feel the intruder in his mind and wondered who it was.

Mortals, ever so ignorant.

Mortals?

Most of them live, I can say the same for those who ate my apple. It said and laughed a wicked laugh.

An apple? The Evil Queen?

No one thinks I’m Halloween scary and for that, they will pay.

Seriously, this was a bad fairy slash, old woman. There was nothing scary about her, she was just an ugly old hag. 

Oh? She said mockingly.

A slender woman with an icy beauty appeared in front of him. She had a poker face and a black veil but that was where the similarities between her and the Disney adaptation ended. She had a black fur coat over a dark shade of emerald green and golden embroidered jacket lehenga. A thick brass crown sat on her head and a raven sat on her shoulder, staring at him with its beady eyes.

Let the fun begin.
