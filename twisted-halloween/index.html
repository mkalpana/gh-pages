Twisted Halloween

By RedEagle3
November 5, 2020
0

Gothel was walking back from the mall when she saw other children her age heading toward the public library. They were the same set of people, every week, every time she walked to get the groceries. And just like every time she debated, would she go over? Would she say hi? No, just like every time she continued walking pretending to pay no attention to the group of girls and boys from her school.

She picked up her pace, she was behind schedule. If she wasn’t home by five, her mother would get worried. She unlocked the door, went in and locked it before calling her mother.

“I’m home, mhm, yes I got the bread. Anything else? Sure.” She ended the landline and slowly unloaded the groceries.

A few hours passed as she read books and studied for her math test, taking note of the time she sprang up and started on her chores, she mopped and vacuumed and did the dishes and baked. After a few cakes or two, she picked up her tablet and began sketching.

For hours she sat there, her stylus moving over the screen in an almost hypnotic pattern. When she finished her masterpiece she screenshotted it and uploaded it to the internet to be viewed by millions of people across the world. This was her only way of communicating other than that occasional conversation at school.

Uploading art was her thing, and the more she did it the more she liked it. She felt connected to others and it also made her feel a bit like a rebel. Like she was secretly leading a secret life. But if she put it that way it did sound so special anymore. Her mother worked at this fashion company and was rarely not in contact with her. She was usually overseas at places organising events. She walked over to the balcony and opened it.

She was looking down as the evening breeze moved through her hair making it fly out behind her in all directions. There were more than five hundred feet below her and there was this sense of déjà vu. Stretching she grabbed her brush and got ready to brush down her curly, raven, black hair.

She brushed and brushed and brushed some more. Still stuck in the same place, wondering and wondering when her life would begin.

“Honey!” A voice shouted from outside, outside her world.

“Comming!” She strode to the door the furniture snagging on some of her hair. She bundling up her dark hair and opened the door for her mother.

A few moments later a lady in light purple robe entered the room and lowered her hood. A mane of blond locks tumbled out and over her shoulders like water would in a waterfall.

“Hello honey, how was school?” Rapunzel asked her in that posh accent.
